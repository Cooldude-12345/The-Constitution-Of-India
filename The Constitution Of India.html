The Constitution Of India

What is a Constitution ?

A Constitution is is an aggregate of fundamental principles or established precedents that constitute the legal basis of a polity, organisation or other type of entity and commonly determine how that entity is to be governed.

Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (14 April 1891 â€“ 6 December 1956) was an Indian jurist, economist, and social reformer who fought economic and social discrimination against the untouchables (now dalits) in India's Hindu society, and who later renounced Hinduism and inspired the Dalit Buddhist movement. Our Fundamental Rights and Duties Under the Indian Constitution

Our Fundatmental Rights

1. Right to Equality
2. Right to Freedom
3. Right against Exploitation
4. Right to Freedom of Religion
5. Right to Life
6. Cultural and Educational Rights
7. Right to Constitutional Remedies
8. Right to Privacy

Our Fundamental Responsibilities

1. To abide by the Constitution and respect it
2. To uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India
3. To promote harmony and brotherhood
4. To preserve the rich heritage of our culture
5. To safeguard public property
6. To strive towards Excellence
7. To protect and improve the natural environment
